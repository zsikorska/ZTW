What are international cuisines?

When it comes to culinary traditions, every country has a unique story to tell. From the spices used to the cooking techniques employed, food is one of the most important ways in which culture is expressed. International cuisines refer to the cooking traditions and practices of various regions and countries around the world. They are characterized by unique ingredients, cooking methods, and cultural influences, which contribute to the distinct flavors and textures of each cuisine.

Portugal

Portugal is a country in southwestern Europe known for its rich maritime heritage and diverse culinary traditions. Its cuisine is heavily influenced by its colonial past and Mediterranean influences.

Characteristics

  • Seafood is consumed in large quantities.
  • Highly influenced by the colonial era, Mediterranean influences are also found.
  • Spices like peri-peri are widely used.
  • Herbs like bay leaves and parsley are important.
  • Olive oil is the basis of most dishes.

Typical dishes

Francesinha

Caldo Verde

Bacalhau com Natas

Pastel de Nata

Ovos Moles

Italy

Italy is a country in Southern Europe known for its rich history, art, and culinary traditions. Its cuisine is renowned for its simple yet bold flavors. Olive oil is a major cooking medium and flavor enhancer, and wine is a common accompaniment to meals.

Characteristics

  • Coffee, specifically espresso, is served after meals.
  • Italian meals are accompanied by wine.
  • Olive oil is a major cooking medium, and it is also used as a flavoring agent.
  • Starch-based products like pasta, pizza, and bread are the major source of carbohydrates.

Typical dishes

Pizza

Spaghetti Carbonara

Tiramisu
